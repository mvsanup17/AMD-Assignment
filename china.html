Top 3 Places in China



The Great Wall of China


The Great Wall of China is one of the most iconic landmarks in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage site. Stretching over 13,000 miles, it was originally built to protect Chinese states and empires from invading forces. The Wall winds through various terrains, including mountains, deserts, and grasslands. Popular sections for visitors include Badaling, Mutianyu, and Jinshanling, each offering stunning views and a glimpse into the ancient architectural marvel.



The Forbidden City, Beijing


The Forbidden City, located in the heart of Beijing, served as the imperial palace for 24 emperors during the Ming and Qing dynasties. This vast complex covers 180 acres and consists of 980 surviving buildings. As a symbol of China's political and ceremonial center, it is a masterpiece of Chinese architecture, with grand halls, intricately designed gardens, and extensive courtyards. Today, it houses the Palace Museum, which showcases a vast collection of Chinese art and historical artifacts.



Terracotta Army, Xi'an


The Terracotta Army is a collection of terracotta sculptures depicting the armies of Qin Shi Huang, the first Emperor of China. Discovered in 1974 by local farmers near Xi'an, this UNESCO World Heritage site includes thousands of life-sized figures of warriors, horses, and chariots buried with the emperor to protect him in the afterlife. Each figure is unique, with distinct facial expressions and details. The site is part of the larger Mausoleum of the First Qin Emperor, which remains an active archaeological area.
